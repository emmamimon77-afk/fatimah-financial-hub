What It Does

Net Present Value (NPV) calculates the value of future cash flows in today's money minus your initial investment. Internal Rate of Return (IRR) finds the annual growth rate your project is expected to generate.

Why It Matters

NPV tells you if a project adds value (positive = good, negative = reconsider). IRR helps compare projects - the higher the rate, the better the return. Both account for the time value of money - a dollar today is worth more than a dollar tomorrow.

Key Insights

Accept projects when NPV > 0 or IRR > discount rate. Payback period shows how quickly you recover your investment. Profitability Index > 1 means the project creates value. For Shariah-compliant investing, ensure cash flows come from permissible sources.
