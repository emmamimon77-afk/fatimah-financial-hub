What It Does

Break-even analysis determines the point where total revenue equals total costs - the moment your business starts making profit. It shows how many units you must sell to cover all fixed and variable expenses.

Why It Matters

Knowing your break-even point helps set sales targets, price products appropriately, and understand your cost structure. It's essential for business planning, loan applications, and making informed decisions about expansion.

Key Insights

Lower break-even points mean less risk. Increase contribution margin by raising prices or reducing variable costs. Fixed costs should be minimized when starting out. Monitor your margin of safety - how far sales can fall before losing money.
