What It Does

ROI measures the efficiency of an investment by comparing the gain or loss to its cost. Simple ROI = (Net Return ÷ Cost) × 100%. Annualized ROI accounts for time, letting you compare investments of different durations.

Why It Matters

ROI helps you compare different investment opportunities, evaluate past decisions, and set performance targets. A positive ROI means you're making money; negative means you're losing. Higher ROI indicates better investment efficiency.

Key Insights

Consider time value - a 50% ROI over 1 year beats 100% over 5 years. Factor in all costs, not just purchase price. For Shariah-compliant investing, ensure the underlying business activities are permissible.
