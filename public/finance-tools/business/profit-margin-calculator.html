What It Does

Profit margins measure how much money a business keeps from each dollar of revenue. Gross margin shows product profitability, operating margin reflects operational efficiency, and net margin reveals overall profitability after all expenses.

Why It Matters

Margins help you price products correctly, control costs, and benchmark against competitors. Rising margins indicate improving efficiency; falling margins signal potential problems. Investors use margins to assess business health.

Key Insights

Gross margin should be high enough to cover operating expenses. Operating margin shows management effectiveness. Net margin is the bottom line - what you actually keep. Compare your margins to industry averages for context.
