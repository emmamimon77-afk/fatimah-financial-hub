How The Calculations Work

Ijara (Islamic Lease) Formula:

Monthly Rent = (Asset Value × Annual Rental Rate) ÷ 12

Example: 100,000 × 10% = 10,000/year ÷ 12 = 833.33/month

Total Rent = Monthly Rent × Lease Term (months)

Example: 833.33 × 60 = 50,000

Purchase Option = Asset Value × Purchase Option Percentage

Example: 100,000 × 10% = 10,000

Total Ijara Cost = Total Rent + Purchase Option

Example: 50,000 + 10,000 = 60,000

Note: Security deposit is refundable, so not included in total cost

Conventional Lease Formula:

Monthly Payment Formula (PMT):

PMT = P × [r(1+r)^n] ÷ [(1+r)^n - 1]

Where:
P = Financed Amount (Asset Value - Residual)
r = Monthly Interest Rate (Annual Rate ÷ 12)
n = Number of Payments (Lease Term)

Total Cost = (Monthly Payment × Lease Term) + Residual + Fees

Why Ijara Might Show Lower Cost:

1. Ijara rental rates are often competitive with conventional rates

2. Ijara includes maintenance in rental (conventional may not)

3. Ijara purchase option is typically nominal (1-10% of asset value)

4. Conventional leases often have higher effective rates due to fees

Understanding Ijara: Islamic Lease Principles

What is Ijara?

Ijara (إجارة) is an Islamic leasing contract where the lessor (owner) leases an asset to the lessee for a specified rental payment. Unlike conventional leases:

  • Prohibits interest (riba)
  • Transfers usufruct (benefit) not ownership
  • Includes maintenance responsibility for lessor
  • Often includes lease-to-own option (Ijara wa Iqtina)

Key Differences

Ownership Risk: In Ijara, the lessor bears all ownership risks. In conventional leases, risks are often transferred to the lessee.

Maintenance: Ijara requires the lessor to maintain the asset. Conventional leases typically make the lessee responsible.

Late Payments: Ijara prohibits penalty interest (only charity payments allowed). Conventional leases charge penalty interest.

Sharia Compliance

Ijara must comply with these principles:

  • Asset must exist and be specified
  • Rental must be fixed and known in advance
  • Lessor bears all ownership costs and risks
  • No interest or uncertainty (gharar) in contract
