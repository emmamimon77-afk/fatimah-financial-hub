Core Islamic Finance Principles

No Riba (Interest)

Prohibition of usury and interest-based transactions. Money cannot generate money without productive activity.

Risk-Sharing

Profits and losses must be shared between parties. No guaranteed returns without risk assumption.

Transparency

Full disclosure of costs, risks, and profits. No hidden terms or ambiguous contracts.

Asset-Backed

All financing must be tied to real, tangible assets. No speculation on non-existent goods.

Social Welfare

Financial activities must benefit society. Prohibition of investments in harmful industries.

Ethical Screening

Strict screening against alcohol, gambling, weapons, and other prohibited activities.
