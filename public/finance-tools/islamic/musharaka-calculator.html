How The Calculations Work

Musharaka Calculation Formulas

Total Venture Value

Total = Capital × (1 + r)n

Where:
Capital = Total partner contributions
r = Expected annual return
n = Venture period (years)

Profit Distribution

Partner A = Profit × Ratio A
Partner B = Profit × Ratio B

Note: Profit ratio can be different from capital ratio (agreed upfront)

Loss Distribution

Loss A = Loss × (Capital A ÷ Total)
Loss B = Loss × (Capital B ÷ Total)

Sharia Requirement: Losses MUST be shared by capital ratio

Understanding Musharaka: Islamic Joint Venture Partnership

What is Musharaka?

Musharaka (مشاركة) is an Islamic joint venture partnership where two or more parties contribute capital to a business venture. Unlike Mudaraba, all partners contribute capital and all may participate in management. Profits are shared according to a pre-agreed ratio, while losses are shared strictly in proportion to capital contribution.

"Profit is shared as agreed, loss is borne according to capital contribution." - AAOIFI Sharia Standard

Key Musharaka Principles

Capital Contribution
All partners contribute capital (cash or kind). Capital can be unequal.
Profit Sharing
Profit ratio can be different from capital ratio (agreed upfront).
Loss Sharing
Losses are shared strictly by capital ratio (not by profit ratio).
Management
All partners can participate in management, or some can be sleeping partners.

Musharaka Calculation Formulas

Total Venture Value:

Total = Capital × (1 + Return Rate)Years

Example: 250,000 × (1.12)3 = 351,232

Total Profit/Loss:

Profit = Total - Total Capital

Example: 351,232 - 250,000 = 101,232 profit

Profit Distribution:

Partner A = Total Profit × Profit Ratio A
Partner B = Total Profit × Profit Ratio B

Example (60:40): 101,232 × 60% = 60,739
101,232 × 40% = 40,493

Loss Distribution (Mandatory):

Loss A = Total Loss × (Capital A ÷ Total Capital)
Loss B = Total Loss × (Capital B ÷ Total Capital)

Example (150k:100k): 50,000 × 60% = 30,000
50,000 × 40% = 20,000

Critical Insight:
In Musharaka, losses must be shared strictly in proportion to capital contribution — this is a binding Sharia principle. Profits can be shared in any ratio agreed by the partners. This protects all partners from unfair loss allocation.
