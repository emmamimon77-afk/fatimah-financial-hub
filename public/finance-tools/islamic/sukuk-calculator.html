﷽ Understanding Sukuk (Islamic Bonds)

What is Sukuk?

Sukuk (صكوك, plural of صك "sakk") are Islamic financial certificates that represent ownership in tangible assets, services, or projects. Unlike conventional bonds that represent debt with interest (riba), Sukuk provide returns from underlying asset performance, making them Sharia-compliant.

Types of Sukuk Structures

﷽ Ijara Sukuk

Lease-based sukuk where certificates represent ownership of leased assets. Returns come from rental payments. Most common type (~40% of global sukuk).

﷽ Mudaraba Sukuk

Profit-sharing partnership where one party provides capital, other provides expertise. Returns based on actual business profits.

﷽ Musharaka Sukuk

Joint venture partnership where all parties contribute capital and share profits/losses proportionally.

﷽ Wakala Sukuk

Agency-based structure where investors appoint an agent to manage investments for a specified fee.

  • • Green Sukuk: Increasing popularity for funding renewable energy and sustainable projects.
  • • Risk Profile: Sukuk typically have lower volatility than conventional bonds due to asset backing.
