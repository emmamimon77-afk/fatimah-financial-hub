Understanding Murabaha: Islamic Finance Principles

What is Murabaha?

Murabaha (مُرَابَحَة) is an Islamic financing structure where a financial institution purchases an asset and sells it to the customer at a cost-plus-profit margin. Unlike conventional loans, there is no interest (riba) charged, making it Sharia-compliant.

The term comes from the Arabic root "رَبْح" (profit), emphasizing transparent profit-sharing rather than interest-based lending.

Key Islamic Finance Principles

No Riba (Interest)
Fixed profit margin disclosed upfront, no compound interest
Asset-Backed
Financing tied to real, tangible assets
Transparency
All costs, risks, and profits disclosed upfront
Risk-Sharing
Bank bears ownership risk until sale completion
