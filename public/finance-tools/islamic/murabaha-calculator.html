How The Calculations Work

Murabaha (Islamic Cost-Plus) Formula:

Bank Profit = Asset Cost × Profit Margin

Example: 250,000 × 9% = د.إ22,500

Sale Price = Asset Cost + Bank Profit

Example: 250,000 + 22,500 = د.إ272,500

Financed Amount = Sale Price - Down Payment

Example: 272,500 - 50,000 = د.إ222,500

Monthly Payment = Financed Amount ÷ Number of Months

Example: 222,500 ÷ 60 = د.إ3,708.33/month

Total Fees = Processing Fee + Insurance + Stamp Duty

Example: 1,500 + (1,200 × 5 years) + 800 = د.إ8,300

Total Murabaha Cost = Sale Price + Total Fees

Example: 272,500 + 8,300 = د.إ280,800

Net Cost to Buyer = Total Cost - Down Payment = د.إ230,800

Conventional Loan Formula:

Monthly Payment Formula (PMT):

PMT = P × [r(1+r)^n] ÷ [(1+r)^n - 1]

Where:
P = Loan Principal (250,000 - 50,000 down = 200,000)
r = Monthly Interest Rate (4.5% ÷ 12 = 0.375%)
n = Number of Payments (60 months)

Example: 200,000 × [0.00375(1.00375)^60] ÷ [(1.00375)^60 - 1] = د.إ3,727.57/month

Note: Actually shows د.إ4,660.75 because includes insurance & fees in monthly

Total Interest = (Monthly Payment × Months) - Principal

Example: (4,660.75 × 60) - 200,000 = د.إ79,645 interest

Total Conventional Cost = Total Payments + Fees

Example: 279,645 + 8,800 = د.إ288,445 total

Key Difference:

Murabaha: Fixed profit known upfront, no compound interest

Conventional: Compound interest, rate can change

In this example: Conventional saves د.إ1,154.71 (0.4%)

Understanding Murabaha: Islamic Finance Principles

What is Murabaha?

Murabaha (مُرَابَحَة) is an Islamic financing structure where a financial institution purchases an asset and sells it to the customer at a cost-plus-profit margin. Unlike conventional loans, there is no interest (riba) charged, making it Sharia-compliant.

The term comes from the Arabic root "رَبْح" (profit), emphasizing transparent profit-sharing rather than interest-based lending.

Key Islamic Finance Principles

No Riba (Interest)
Fixed profit margin disclosed upfront, no compound interest
Asset-Backed
Financing tied to real, tangible assets
Transparency
All costs, risks, and profits disclosed upfront
Risk-Sharing
Bank bears ownership risk until sale completion
