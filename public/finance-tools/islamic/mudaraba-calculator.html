Understanding Mudaraba: Islamic Profit-Sharing Partnership

What is Mudaraba?

Mudaraba (مضاربة) is an Islamic investment partnership where one party provides capital (Rab-ul-Maal / investor) and another party provides expertise and management (Mudarib / manager). Profits are shared according to a pre-agreed ratio, while financial losses are borne solely by the capital provider.

Mudaraba is based on the principle of shared risk and reward, not guaranteed returns. The investor trusts the manager's expertise, and both share in the success of the venture.

Key Mudaraba Principles

Capital Provider
Investor provides 100% capital, bears all financial loss
Manager (Mudarib)
Provides expertise, management, bears effort/time loss
Profit Sharing
Pre-agreed ratio (e.g., 70% investor, 30% manager)
Loss Distribution
Financial loss: 100% investor, Effort loss: 100% manager

Mudaraba Calculation Formulas

Total Value Formula:

Total = Capital × (1 + Profit Rate)Years

Example: 100,000 × (1.10)5 = 161,051

Total Profit:

Profit = Total - Capital

Example: 161,051 - 100,000 = 61,051

Profit Sharing:

Investor Share = Profit × Investor Ratio
Manager Share = Profit × Manager Ratio

Example: 61,051 × 70% = 42,736
61,051 × 30% = 18,315

Investor Net Return:

Net = Investor Share - Fees
Return % = (Net ÷ Capital) × 100

Example: 42,736 - 0 = 42,736
Return: 42,736 ÷ 100,000 = 42.7%

Key Insight:
Mudaraba is not simply an "Islamic loan" — it's a genuine partnership. The investor shares in the venture's success, not just receiving fixed returns. This creates a more equitable relationship and aligns with Islamic principles of justice and risk-sharing.
