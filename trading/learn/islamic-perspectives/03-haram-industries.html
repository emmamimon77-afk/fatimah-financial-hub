🎯 Ethical Screening

Muslims cannot invest in companies involved in activities prohibited by Shariah. This requires careful screening of business activities beyond just avoiding interest.

🍷
Alcohol
Production, distribution, or sale of alcoholic beverages. Includes breweries, distilleries, bars, and liquor stores.
⛔ HARAM
🎰
Gambling
Casinos, betting companies, lottery tickets, sports betting apps, and any business where gambling is primary.
⛔ HARAM
🐷
Pork
Production, processing, and sale of pork products. Includes pig farming, slaughterhouses, and restaurants serving pork.
⛔ HARAM
⚔️
Weapons
Conventional weapons, firearms, ammunition, and military equipment manufacturers (controversial).
⛔ HARAM
🎮
Haram Entertainment
Pornography, music with immoral content, adult entertainment, and any media promoting haram activities.
⛔ HARAM
🏦
Interest-Based
Conventional banks, insurance companies, and any financial institution dealing primarily in interest.
⛔ HARAM

✅ Halal Sectors to Consider

💻 Technology ⚕️ Healthcare 📚 Education 🥩 Halal Food ☀️ Renewable Energy 🕌 Islamic Finance

📊 Shariah Screening Criteria

100%
Business Screening

Primary business must be halal. No involvement in prohibited activities.

< 33%
Debt Ratio

Debt levels below 33% of market capitalization.

< 33%
Liquid Assets

Cash and receivables below 33% of total assets.

🔍 Detailed Screening

  • Primary Business: Haram if >5% revenue from prohibited activities
  • Debt Ratio: Total debt / Market cap < 33%
  • Interest Income: Cash + interest-bearing securities / Market cap < 33%
  • Impure Income: Revenue from haram activities < 5% of total revenue

🔍 Case Studies: Halal or Haram?

Case 1: Tech Giant (Apple, Microsoft)

Business: Technology products, some cash holdings earn interest.

Screening: Primary business is halal. Check if interest income < 5% and debt < 33%.

Ruling: Usually halal with purification (donate the small interest portion).

Case 2: Hotel Chain

Business: Hotels that serve alcohol and have casinos.

Screening: If alcohol revenue > 5%, it's haram. Some scholars allow if separate accounting.

Ruling: Most scholars say avoid entirely if any alcohol served on premises.

Case 3: Airlines

Business: Transportation, but serve alcohol on flights.

Screening: Alcohol is incidental service, not primary business.

Ruling: Permitted by some scholars, avoided by others. If alcohol revenue < 5%, may be permissible.

🌍 Regional Differences in Screening

  • AAOIFI (International): Most strict - 5% threshold for impure income, 30% for debt.
  • S&P Shariah: Uses 33% thresholds for debt and interest income.
  • FTSE/Yasaar: Similar to S&P but excludes more controversial stocks.
  • Local Scholars: May have stricter rulings based on local context.
