Order Types

🎯 Learning Objectives

  • Understand market, limit, and stop orders
  • Know when to use each order type
  • Calculate position sizes with stop-loss
  • Understand Islamic perspective on each order
  • Practice with real scenarios
📊
Market Order
Executes immediately at the current market price.
✓ Guaranteed execution
✓ Instant fills
✗ No price control
✗ Slippage in volatile markets
Example: AAPL at $175.50 → market order buys at $175.50 (or very close)
⚡
Limit Order
Executes only at your specified price or better.
✓ Price control
✓ No slippage
✗ May not execute
✗ Can miss opportunities
Example: AAPL at $175.50 → limit order at $174.00 fills only if price drops
🛡️
Stop-Loss Order
Becomes a market order when price hits your stop price.
✓ Limits losses
✓ Automatic protection
✗ Can trigger on temporary dips
✗ Slippage possible
Example: Buy AAPL at $175.50 → stop-loss at $170.00 triggers if price drops

📌 Advanced Order Types

Stop-Limit Order

Combines stop-loss and limit order. When price hits the stop, it places a limit order instead of a market order.

Example: Stock at $50. Set stop at $48 and limit at $47.90. If price hits $48, a limit order to sell at $47.90 is placed.

Trailing Stop

Stop price moves with the stock price. Locks in profits as price rises, but still protects if price falls.

Example: Set 5% trailing stop on stock at $100. If stock rises to $110, stop rises to $104.50. If stock drops from $110 to $104.50, it triggers.

🕌 Islamic Perspective

✅ Permissible Orders

  • Market Orders: Generally permissible as they represent immediate trade
  • Limit Orders: Permissible as a promise to trade at a specified price
  • Stop-Loss: Permissible as a risk management tool

⚠️ What to Avoid

  • Margin trading (involves riba/interest)
  • Short selling (selling what you don't own)
  • Derivatives with excessive uncertainty (gharar)
  • Day trading with extreme frequency

📜 Hadith Reference

The Prophet Muhammad (ﷺ) said: "The two parties in a transaction have the right to cancel it as long as they have not separated." (Bukhari)

Application: Limit orders respect this by giving you control over the execution price.

📐 Position Size Calculation

Position Size = (Account × Risk %) ÷ (Entry - Stop)

Example: $10,000 Account, 2% Risk

Entry: $175.50
Stop Loss: $170.00
Risk per share: $5.50
Position Size: ($10,000 × 0.02) ÷ $5.50 = 36 shares

📋 Key Takeaways

  • Market orders execute immediately but lack price control
  • Limit orders give price control but may not execute
  • Stop-loss orders protect capital automatically
  • Always calculate position size before entering a trade
  • Islamic trading prohibits margin, short selling, and excessive speculation
