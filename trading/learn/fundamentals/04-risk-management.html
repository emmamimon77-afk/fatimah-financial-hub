Risk Management

Risk management is preparing for what may come - a principle of wisdom in all endeavors.

🎯 Learning Objectives

  • Understand why risk management is the most important skill
  • Learn position sizing using the 1-2% rule
  • Master risk/reward ratios (aim for 1:3)
  • Understand diversification across sectors and assets
  • Apply Islamic principles to risk management

💰 The Golden Rule: Never Risk More Than 1-2% Per Trade

Position Size = (Account × Risk %) ÷ (Entry - Stop Loss)

📊 Example: $10,000 Account, 2% Risk

Entry: $100
Stop Loss: $95 (5% risk per share)
Risk per share: $5
Max Risk: $10,000 × 0.02 = $200
Position Size: $200 ÷ $5 = 40 shares

If the trade fails, you lose $200 (2% of account), not your entire capital!

⚠️ Why This Matters

If you lose 50% of your account, you need a 100% gain just to break even. Protecting capital is more important than making profit. A series of small losses is survivable; one big loss is not.

📈 Risk/Reward Ratio: Aim for 1:3

Risk/Reward = (Entry - Stop) ÷ (Target - Entry)

📊 Good Example

Entry: $50
Stop: $48 (Risk $2)
Target: $56 (Reward $6)
Ratio: 1:3 ✅

📊 Poor Example

Entry: $50
Stop: $49 (Risk $1)
Target: $51 (Reward $1)
Ratio: 1:1 ❌

🧠 Why It Matters

With a 1:3 risk/reward ratio, you can be wrong 2 out of 3 times and still make money. Risk management is about survival and consistent growth, not perfection.

🛡️ Risk Management Tools

✅ Stop-Loss Orders

Automatic exit at a predetermined price. Your best friend in trading.

✅ Trailing Stops

Lock in profits as price moves in your favor. Moves up with the price.

✅ Position Sizing

Calculate shares based on account size and risk per trade.

✅ Diversification

Spread risk across different sectors and asset classes.

❌ Common Risk Management Mistakes

  • No stop-loss: Hoping a losing trade will reverse (gambler's mentality)
  • Moving stop-loss lower: Increasing risk when already losing
  • Overleveraging: Trading too large for your account size
  • Averaging down: Adding to losing positions
  • Chasing losses: Trying to recover losses with larger trades

🕌 Islamic Perspective on Risk Management

The Prophet Muhammad (ﷺ) said: "Trust in Allah, but tie your camel." (Tirmidhi)

Risk management is tying your camel - taking practical precautions while placing your ultimate trust in Allah. It is not a lack of faith, but wisdom and responsibility.

Islamic principles in risk management:

  • Do not risk what you cannot afford to lose
  • Avoid gambling-like behavior (maysir)
  • Be responsible stewards of your wealth (khalifah)
  • Balance hope in Allah's provision with practical preparation

📋 Key Takeaways

  • Risk 1-2% per trade: Never risk more than you can afford to lose
  • Aim for 1:3 risk/reward: You can be wrong 2/3 of the time and still profit
  • Use stop-loss orders: Always have a predetermined exit point
  • Diversify: Spread risk across sectors and asset classes
  • Islamic wisdom: Trust in Allah, but tie your camel
