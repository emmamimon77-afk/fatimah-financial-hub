📊 What is Technical Analysis?

Technical analysis is the study of market action through charts. The belief is that all known information is already reflected in the price, and history tends to repeat itself.

Three Core Principles

  • Market discounts everything - All news, earnings, emotions are in the price
  • Price moves in trends - Once a trend starts, it tends to continue
  • History repeats itself - Human psychology creates recognizable patterns

Human Psychology

Markets are driven by two emotions: Fear and Greed. Technical analysis helps us recognize these emotions in price action.

📉 Support & Resistance

🛡️ Support Level

A price level where buying pressure is strong enough to prevent the price from falling further. Think of it as a "floor" under the price.

Example: AAPL bounces off $150 three times → $150 is support.

⛰️ Resistance Level

A price level where selling pressure is strong enough to prevent the price from rising further. Think of it as a "ceiling" above the price.

Example: AAPL hits $175 three times and falls back → $175 is resistance.

🔄 Role Reversal

Once a resistance level is broken, it often becomes new support. Once a support level is broken, it often becomes new resistance.

📈 Trend Lines & Channels

Uptrend

Higher highs and higher lows. Draw trend line connecting the higher lows.

Downtrend

Lower highs and lower lows. Draw trend line connecting the lower highs.

How to Draw Trend Lines:

  • Identify at least 2 touch points (3 is stronger)
  • In an uptrend, connect the lowest points (lows)
  • In a downtrend, connect the highest points (highs)
  • Extend the line to see future potential reactions

📊 Common Chart Patterns

📉📈
Head & Shoulders

Reversal pattern. Three peaks with middle highest.

📈📉
Double Bottom

Bullish reversal. Price tests support twice.

📉📈
Double Top

Bearish reversal. Price tests resistance twice.

📊
Flags

Continuation pattern. Brief pause before continuing.

🔺
Triangles

Consolidation before breakout.

🌊
Wedge

Converging trend lines. Can signal reversal or continuation.

🕌 Islamic Perspective

Technical analysis is a tool, like a stethoscope for a doctor. It doesn't predict the unseen future (ghayb), but helps analyze current market conditions. As long as one believes all outcomes are from Allah, using tools for analysis is permissible.

— Sh. Mufti Taqi Usmani

✅ Permissible

  • Using TA to identify trends
  • Risk management
  • Finding entry points

⚠️ Not Permissible

  • Believing patterns "guarantee" future moves
  • Treating charts as deterministic
  • Obsessive or gambling-like behavior

✍️ Practice Scenario

You're looking at AAPL stock. It has bounced off $150 three times in the last month. Each time it reaches $175, sellers push it down. Where would you consider buying? Where would you consider taking profits?

Suggested Answer: Consider buying near support ($150) with a stop-loss slightly below. Consider taking profits near resistance ($175). This gives a favorable risk/reward ratio.

📋 Key Takeaways

  • Support = price floor where buyers step in
  • Resistance = price ceiling where sellers step in
  • Trends are your friend - trade in their direction
  • Patterns help identify potential reversals or continuations
  • Islamic view: TA is a permissible tool, not fortune-telling
